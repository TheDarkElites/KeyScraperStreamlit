Ordering is closed.

Running my first GB for the community and trying to put this board to fruition, here is TaiChi, a TKL made by Eve.
Please refer to the Interest Check here: https://geekhack.org/index.php?topic=97582.0

This oriental looking board [TaiChi] is heavily inspired by the philosophy of Taichi, a Chinese cosmological term for the "Supreme Ultimate" state of undifferentiated absolute and infinite potential, the oneness before duality, from which Yin and Yang originate, can be compared with the old 無極. The word 太極 comes from I Ching: "易有太極，是生兩儀，兩儀生四象，四象生八卦，八卦定吉凶，吉凶生大業。

I've been a longtime lurker of GH, and signed up just to make this post, as this is a project I've been most excited about.

I'm concerned with the new Yin-Yang design. I love it in concept, but I don't think it is properly executed. The old design conformed to the correct shape/proportions of a standard Yin-Yang, but the new one does not. I also don't think the design concept (making the two parts convex) interferes with adhering to this design.

Here's an image I found of the standard proportions of the Yin-Yang:


To make this properly convex, the following transformations from this would need to happen:

1. Increase the size of the left/right circles.
2. If constrained to 2D, these would overlap. Instead, reconnect them in the middle by angling them down/inward, keeping the edges touching the main big circle locked
3. The rest of each white/black area should continue at the same angle as their respective circles
4. The dots should be at the center of their respective circles.

And that's it. If I knew how to use a 3d modeling program, I would make this. Maybe somebody can help.

Right now this is the only thing that's keeping me from buying what would otherwise be an absolute masterpiece.

Polygon

After over a year of development, 4 prototypes and a lot of money spent I am now ready to put Polygon up for a GB. To kick things off I’ll show you some pictures of the prototypes, followed by some general information, such as pricing, and what you can expect to receive for your money. I’ll finish things off by giving you a timeline, an idea of the bits and pieces you need to assemble Polygon and a FAQ.






Prototype pictures:










Proto gallery: https://imgur.com/a/6Uny3Fy



Pricing and where to buy:

• Polygon including PCB: 449€ (530 USD) + shipping
• Extra PCB: 67.8 € (80 USD)
• Extra plate: 25.5 € (30 USD
• Foam (ISO or ANSI, with foam for chambers): 20 € (23.6 USD)
• Brass knob: 17€ (20.06 USD)

Where to buy:

• My website: https://rotwitt.com
• For news: https://discord.gg/kQbxZnJdfF




What is included?


Case

• Top
• Middle
• Bottom
• Brass weight
• Brass plate (ISO or ANSI)
• Aluminium knob
• PCB (ISO or ANSI)
• Linear bearings
• Washer
• Screws (Obviously
• Custom molded feet
• Custom molded gasket

Extras

• Foam
• Brass knob




Available colours:



• North sea blue
• Forest green
• Silver
• Plum
• Rose Gold
• Gunmetal




Case Specifications

• Typing angle: 7°
• Case material: Aluminium 6061
• Case finishing: Sandblasted and anodized
• Case colour: Silver, Gunmetal, Forest green, North sea blue, Rose gold, Plum
• Weight material: Brass
• Weight finishing: Sandblasting
• Approximate weight: 3,2 kg (8.37 lbs)
• Custom knob (Extra)

Plate:

• Plate material: Brass
• Plate thickness: 1,5mm
• Plate finish: Sandblasted

PCB specifications:


• Layout support: ISO or ANSI
• Finish: ENIG and Custom PCB art
• Colour: Matte black
• Hotswap
• Rotary encoder (PEC11L)
• Per key RGB
• AT Mega 32 MCU
• USB-C daughterboard
• QMK
• VIA





Collabs:

KEEBSTUFF:



Keebstuff Kabelmanufaktur has made a highe-end custom lemo cable, with brass sleeving that matches the Plum Polygon colourway perfectly!
They are currently holding a giveaway for a cable, which you can check out here: https://www.reddit.com/r/mechmarket/comments/pctjvf/bulk_custom_cables_by_keebstuff_polygon_cable/.
They also offer a made-to-order service that makes it possible for you to colour-match any keeb you'd like!
https://keebstuff.com/



Timeline:


The GB will launch the 20th of August, will run for 21 days. Until the 10th of September, to allow you to make changes to your order, or order any additonal extras. The GB will be FCFS.

• GB Start: 20th of august at 23:59 GMT +2
• GB end: 10th of september 23:59 GMT +2
• 5 months delivery to me
• 2 months of me shipping out the packages internationally
• Every package should be shipped by the 31st of March 2022




What do I need for assembly?

• A set of allen keys with the following sizes: H2.5, H2.0, H1.5, H1.3
• 66 switches of your choice, and the patience to straighten each and every one of their pins
• Your favorite keycaps
• PCB-mount stabilizers
• A song that makes you feel good
• The assembly guide




FAQ

Q: Firmware?
A: QMK w. VIA or VIAL support

Q: Will you add more plates?
A: No, I'm trying to keep complexity down. Plate files will be available on my website post GB

Q: Will you add more colors?
A: No

Q: Where can I participate in the GB?
A: My website: https://Rotwitt.com

Q: Where will you ship from?:
A: Denmark, in the EU

Q: Will you be using any vendors?:
A: I don't plan to

Q: How many will be available?
A: 500 units will be available at this time. I might run another round, if there's demand for it

Q: Will there be a build stream?
A: Hipyotech and Apiary will receive a prototype to build

Q: Who will be shipping the keyboard?
A: DHL

Q: Will you be declaring customs?
A: Yes.

Q: Do you have a e-mailing list?
A: Not currently. Please let me know if you want emails and I'll consider setting it up.

“Hey man. I totally get it. It's pretty expensive, no doubt about that. 
Back in january when I got the first quote I set a price target of ~430 USD, which I was pretty happy with, but unfortunately the latest quotation that I got from the manu added 100 USD to the manufacturing costs. Either way, thanks for your interest! I plan to design another, more affordable board in the future ”

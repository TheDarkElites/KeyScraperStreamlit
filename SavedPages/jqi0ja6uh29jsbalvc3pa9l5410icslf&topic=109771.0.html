Hello Everyone! This is the GB for round 2 of the Panc40 keyboard kit.



Group Buy Purchase Page

Geekhack IC

Discord GB channel

This is a 40% keyboard (Minorca: 43 keys, Sebright: 41 keys, Ancona: 47 keys) I designed. Here’s the GB link for the first round’s GB. This is a high profile aluminum milled case, with a PCB supporting hotswap and underglow. The switch plate is going to be top-mounted, with 6 circular tabs for added stability. This will have a left-mounted USB location, and uses USB-C and does support QMK.

UPDATE EDIT: I've received interest in having a new PCB option added, and I've worked with the designer Nasp to have it added as an option for this GB! The PCB is called the Pursuit40, and it is an ortholinear option with some extended bottom row options. It is MX-only and non-hotswap, so it requires soldering. It has a USB-C port and supports QMK. It is perfectly compatible with the Panc40 case, and has been added as an optional accessory. Because of this addition, I've moved the end-date from 12/20 to 1/3. This doesn't negatively impact the delivery date of the final product, so I wanted to give everyone a chance to get in on the GB!

Price is $320 for the below kit:

  • Milled aluminum top frame
  • 4° angle milled aluminum base with Frosted acrylic diffuser
  • Stainless steel topmount switchplate
  • Panc40 PCB
    • Hotswap
    • USB-C PCB terminal
    • QMK firmware
    • RGB underglow
  • 1x 2mm underplate foam
  • 2x 2u PCB stabs
  • Hardware
    • mounting hardware
    • set of rubber feet
  • CONUS shipping and handling

FAQ section:
More
Q: Why did you create a new ortholinear PCB when it mimics layouts provided by the planck PCB?

A: The planck PCB has the USB terminal in a different location, so I couldn’t use it with this case configuration. Also, this PCB adds a few more layout options, as I wanted to make sure it was differentiated a bit.

Q: Why is the incline 4° instead of 5° or 7°?

A: After evaluating different configurations and angle options, I felt that 4 degrees was the best option. I did use 5 and felt it was a bit too drastic for this small of a keyboard. The prototype tester group also felt that the 4° was a suitable middle ground.

Q: Is it possible to only get the ortholinear options without buying the staggered options?

A: Yes!

Q: Is there going to be plate foam available for these layouts?

A: Yes!

Q: Is this build going to include a weight option?

A: No, I’ve never been a big fan of weights in my designs, and I think other people do a much better job than I do at that.

Q: Will the plate files be available for this keyboard?

A: Yes, I've made them available for R1 and while I don't explicitly plan on modifying the design for R2, I want to make sure there aren't any small changes, so I'm going to release the files after the GB ships.

A Timeline of the Panc40 R2 Group Buy


January 4th, 2021

Group buy closes officially.



Even now, the estimated shipping date is advertised as Q2 2021. It is now Q2 2022.


January 5th, 2021

The first update email goes out to group buy participants. Alex claims that the orders should be in the manufacturers' queue before Chinese New Year starts (February 12th, 2021). He also says that updates will be given on Geekhack and email, and he will be available on Discord.






January 22nd, 2021

On Geekhack, Alex says that everything has been ordered and is either actively shipping to him or being worked on. So nothing should be pending a manufacturer starting work on it.




April 13th, 2021

Alex sends out email update #02 to GB participants. Apparently we're waiting on a production sample to ship to him before starting machining of the full group buy order. He links to the Geekhack picture showing plates and brass midlayers. This will be the last time Alex updates the Geekhack GB thread.






May 22nd, 2021

Alex says we're looking at shipping to buyers around June 14th - 28th.




July 1st - 7th, 2021

Apparently MCU shortages have pushed shipping back to end of July. On Discord, he says that the issue is now resolved, but it's caused delay. To my knowledge, this is the first time he's said anything about PCBs being stuck in manufacturing.






August 2nd, 2021

Alex says something abourt working hard to finish PCBs quickly. Not sure if this means that he ordered them without components pre-soldered or what, this is the first time any "work" on the PCBs has been mentioned. No pictures of the PCBs are shown anywhere.




September 13th, 2021

Alex has been absolutely absent on all platforms for the last five weeks. This is one of the weirdest, most bizarre, incomprehensible GB updates I've ever received in my life. For some reason, all participants are cc'd instead of bcc'd. Here's the email sent out, in full:



I don't know where to begin with this. The fact that this is the first anyone's heard about this, the fact that he allegedly made drastic changes to the GB without informing its participants... there are a litany of questionable ethical business decisions on display here. And finally, he says that we should all receive our orders on October 14th. Also this is the last time he posts at all in the 40% server.


October 19th, 2021

October 14th, the promised shipping date, comes and goes with no news on any platform. On October 19th, an email goes out to GB participants that QC is taking longer than expected, and the new shipping date is November 2nd. No pictures or proof have been shared to demonstrate that any of this is true.




November 6th - 17th 2021

This update is deja vu from the previous month. Shipping deadline comes and goes with no messages, a few days later we're told QC and packing are taking longer than expected. We will get an update on Nov. 14th and expect shipping around December 16th.



The update on the 14th comes in on the 17th and once again blames QC with absolutely no additional pictures or information.



Requests for pictures go ignored.


December 28th - 31st, 2021

Interesting, I did not notice that.  For my own part I think ISO kit sales have increased a lot in the past few years.  On the majority of the more popular sets, ISO is normally produced.  In relation to Yugo specifically, it was not really a popular set, so the overall numbers were low, not just the ISO kit.

I use both ISO and ANSI across many boards.  Being born and raised in the UK the majority of keyboards I used (long before this hobby started, in fact back when personal computers started) were ISO'ish in their key layout.  Computers like the Commodore 64, Amiga, BBC, Archimedes and Commodore PC1, PC10.  At the same time the main business computers in use like DEC PDP were mostly ANSI'ish in their keyboard styles.  When actually typing while working I prefer ISO layout and I need UK ISO specifically.  When using some Ergo layouts I prefer ANSI layout usually (apart from the Virgo).  I also want to say that when ISO is offered either included in the base kit (1st choice) or as part of the Norde/UK kit (expensive way) I always buy it with every set of caps.  In fact I support ISO kits every time I buy (and I buy way too many).  I also think that for the benefit of your hobby and the love of keyboard design, everyone should be building both ANSI and ISO layouts, if not then why buy any more than one keyboard of choice.

Introducing the Aalto by Tom, released by Antipode Studio

Specs:
  • Priced at $460 instock, includes carrying case, pcb and aluminium plate(the board is currently priced at 1000 on our website, if you win a spot through the raffle you will receive a discount code)
  • Includes 5052 aluminium plate as standard in a fixed split bs, ansi layout
  • QMK/VIA/VIAL compatible PCB designed by Martin in a fixed split bs, ansi layout
  • Carrying case included
  • 1.2mm PCB.
  • 1.75mm front heigh
  • Typing angle 6.9 degrees
  • Silver / Brass - Clear top and bottom, beadblasted and coated brass weight, navy aluminium plate
  • Black / PVD - Anodized black top and bottom, matte rainbow PVD weight, black aluminium plate

A word from the designer:
Having used a variety of 60s I found myself wanting something in a similar form factor with just a little bit more. Instead of a traditional 65% layout, Aalto features a set of macro keys to the left which I find more convenient in day-to-day use.

We tried a number of small adjustments in prototyping to optimise the typing experience by finding a balance between sound and feeling, and I believe that our final product satisfies this as best as possible. While the board features a lot of the more modern design ideas,  assembly was quite deliberately kept as straightforward as possible -  top mount has always been my preference, and despite the internal fastening design I believe that Aalto is a very easy board to put together.

There’s a lack of visible branding on the board that I believe satisfies the vision of a very high-end typing instrument that’s focused on user experience first and foremost. The name is a nod to architect Alvar Aalto, as well as its Finnish translation to ‘wave’, which is reflected in the undulating corners. I hope you enjoy this keyboard as much as I do.

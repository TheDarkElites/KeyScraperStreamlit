The Maccaron Switch

The manufacturer of the switch is HMX. I am pretty sure a lot of you have never heard of this factory before, but they are a rising star right now. Just like BSUN at the beginning of 2023, you will only believe how good they are once you see their products yourself. At the moment, I am writing this IC post with the Maccaron switches on my keyboard, and I have to say I am quite addicted to them.

Looking at the material choices of the switch, we can see that it comes with a POK stem. The POK material is known for its durability and low surface friction coefficient rate. That ensures the stem is super smooth even at the stock condition with no lube. The PC top housing and nylon bottom housing are much more for the sound of the switch instead for smoothness as the POK stem already had that job done. With a Nylon bottom housing, the bottom-out sound of the Maccaron switch is clacky indeed. However, thanks to the POK stem, the sound is not harsh or sharp. It is a pleasant medium clack that is just right for enjoyment. As for the PC top housing, it emits a crispy and quiet sound during top-out which is actually quite soothing. Overall speaking, the pre-built Maccaron switch with a POK stem is super smooth, and it has just the clack I have been looking for.

How about the box POM stem? Well, to be honest, as it is a box-style POM stem, I did not expect it to be as smooth as the POK stem. I put the POM stem into the switches without lubing them first and had a try. I could both hear and feel a small amount of scratchiness coming out of it, but it was all within the normal range. After lubrication, I could hardly feel there is any difference in smoothness between the POM and POK stem. The conclusion is that if you are worried about the POM stem being not as smooth as the POK stem, you won't have to. All they need is just a thin layer of lube, and they are good to go.
Regarding the sound, the box POM stem sounds much louder and higher pitched than the POK stem. If the POK stem is not clacky enough for you, the POM stem will definitely be your favorite. One thing to note is that the box stem is less wobble than the POK stem of course, but the MX POK stem is super steady already, and stem wobble wouldn't be an issue no matter which stem you choose to go with.

I have said enough of my own opinions about the switch. I will stop now and leave something for future reviews. Please refer back to either this post or the GB post later for review and sound test videos.

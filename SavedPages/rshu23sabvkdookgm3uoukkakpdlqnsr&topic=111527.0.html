The kit will be priced at $375 USD excluding sales/import tax and shipping. A kit includes: 1 x top, 1 x bottom, 1 x plate, and 1 PCB. The angle of the case is 6°. It includes a custom PCB designed by me that supports ISO/ANSI layouts and runs QMK, this will be matte black.

Options:
Top is available in WK or WKL - both have 0.5u arrow blocker
Case is available in Aluminium in 3 colours: E-Black, E-White & Anodised Silver
Plate is available in ISO/ANSI half or full layout in 2 materials: PP (Polypropylene, Black), POM (Polyoxymethylene, White). If the ISO numbers stay low we might have to restrict the material to ensure we hit a suitable price.

FAQ

Won't the plate fall out?
The plate does sit loose in the assembly and if you pick it up and shake it then it will rattle but there's not enough room for it to fall off, in typing this doesn't happen.

What is pin mount? Have there been any other boards that use this mounting style?
there is no pressure from the top half of the case on the plate. There are 4 pins in the corners of the base that hold the plate in place and it rests on the lip of the base. The theory is that the lack of downward pressure allows the plate more freedom of movement towards the center whilst still retaining an even pressure around the edge of the board. Prophet was the first public buy that used it

Which plate material is the best?
Polypropylene benefits the most from this mount style

How much does the keyboard weigh?
The weight of the keyboard built is around 1.1kg (2.5lbs)

Quote from: pathfinder on Tue, 16 March 2021, 20:25:43
Hey Westfoxtrot,

I was hoping you could tell me a bit about the POM vs PP plate options?
Going off of other peoples' sound tests on youtube, the full plate PP option sounds amazing for linear switches.
Would you feel similarly for tactile switches, or recommend a different plate option? And how would you compare the POM to the PP in half or full plate options?

I just entered the groupbuy for the Angel65, but was unsure if I should add a second plate to my order. I already locked in the full plate PP.


I don't use tactiles very often but I know that generally people like a slightly firmer plate for those which is why I included POM. It's a slightly louder sound signature compared to PP in this mount but still works nicely and doesn't bounce like a harder material would (metals/cf etc)

With regards to half and full it's a bit of a difficult one to cover if you haven't used a half plate before. Half plate on this board is a fairly different experience, the relief cuts in the PCB make it a bit more bouncy and floaty of a typing experience - it also has more variation in sound profile between alphas and mods due to the lack of the plate. My overall advice is: if you haven't tried a half plate before then in this board it's really love/hate so might be a bit of a risky move :-)

With regards to half and full it's a bit of a difficult one to cover if you haven't used a half plate before. Half plate on this board is a fairly different experience, the relief cuts in the PCB make it a bit more bouncy and floaty of a typing experience - it also has more variation in sound profile between alphas and mods due to the lack of the plate. My overall advice is: if you haven't tried a half plate before then in this board it's really love/hate so might be a bit of a risky move :-)

Introducing the Lavender KBD67 Lite

Its the KBD67Lite, but lavender; PANTONE 2645U, to be specific. Yes, that is all.

Group Buy January 5- February 15





Pseudo-IC Form


Vendors:

US: Vala Supply
EU: MyKeyboard.eu
CN/Worldwide: KBDFans


Details:

Material:

Injection molded ABS Plastic

Kit Contents:

Hotswap PCB (choice between wired ANSI, wired ISO)

Case (lavender)

PC Plate (injection-molded, either ANSI fixed or ISO fixed)

KBDFans stabilizers, enough for a 60% board

Silicone mute/dampener

Case foam

Coiled USB-C cable

KBD67 Lite Carrying case

KBDfans stabilizer foam stickers

Rubber feet and Screws

Case Only Contents:

Case (lavender)

Shipping:

Estimated to ship late Q1- early Q2 2022





Renders







Dimensions/Specs






Pricing:




Collaboration:

FR4 Plate: Custom Lavender/Gold Bredworks FR4 Plate



Bredworks will be collaborating with us to bring us a never-before-seen lavender plate! It features universal compatibility with both ISO and ANSI layouts, and a sweet gold trim! (Will include necessary standoffs and screws)

   

To keep up to date with development and news about this board, check out the Vala Supply discord!

Did you get permission to run this from the gmk lavender creator? He owns the color for keyboards and keyboards accessories

I'm afraid the odds of Dotnick owning this single color as a trademark in the United States (at least) for keyboards and keyboard accessories is slim to none. I doubt Dotnick could show secondary meaning under U.S. trademark law, which is absolutely required for single-color marks. Trademark law is not first to use, it is first to use in a way that creates secondary meaning (aka distinctiveness, aka the mark is functioning as an indicator of source, rather than being perceived as an aesthetic feature in the relevant market). Single color marks cannot be inherently distinctive.

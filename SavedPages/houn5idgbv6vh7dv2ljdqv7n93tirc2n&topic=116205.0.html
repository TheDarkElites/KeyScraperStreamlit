Haven60 by Garner
Unique & Customizable

Inspired by vintage Victorian furniture, the Haven60 gives a unique impression on the desk with its bold and curvy design.

Haven60 is designed to be compatible with standard 60% PCBs to support a wide range of layouts and allows you to use a PCB of your own preference.
This way the keyboard supports anything from split spacebar and split backspace to arrow keys and HHKB. Both ANSI and ISO.



_________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________





_________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________

     

_________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________


Supported Layouts

PCB plate will be available in aluminum, brass and polycarbonate.




_________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________


Revamped Top Mount Concept

This mounting system provides a combination of gasket mount and top mount.
The idea is that the cylindrical rubber gaskets will allow the board's flex to be adjustable.
More details and showcase further below.




_________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________


Group buy info

This keyboard is sold without PCB and comes with a plate that supports a wide range of layouts for maximum customizability.
Make sure your PCB supports the listed layouts above and have USB-C placed at the top left corner (the GH60/dz60/pok3r standard).
I have compiled a list of compatible PCBs further below, (and I'm sure there are more).

There is also a matching custom wrist rest to be offered as well as a carrying case with cutout for both the keyboard and the wrist rest!

Flex configuration

Haven60's flexibility can be configured by simply adjusting the tightening of the plate screws, below are a few examples to show the span of possible flex.
Going for high flex requires you to skip case foam. Album here

The plate is allowed to move vertically thanks to the synergy between cylindrical rubber gaskets on the screws and the top frame gasket.
This means there is less stress applied to the PCB as it isn't being bent from fixed points.


Maximum flex ~3mm screw distance (m3 screw as reference) | Polycarbonate plate | PCB foam | No bottom case foam




Low flex ~1.5mm screw distance (half m3 screw as reference) | Polycarbonte Plate | PCB foam | No bottom case foam





No flex Screws fastened just until you start gettting a bit of resistance | Polycarbonate Plate | PCB foam | Bottom case foam

FAQ and other information

Does this keyboard include PCB?
No, the keyboard is designed to fit the standard 60% PCB's like dz60/gh60 with USB-C in the upper left corner.
Several PCBs have been tested during the prototype stage and I made a list of those that are confirmed compatible:

DZ60 rev. 3
DZ60 RGB v2 Hot Swap
BM60 RGB Hot swap
XD60

For HHKB with split spacebar these will likely fit:
H60 by Hineybush
NOP60 by Checkerboards

Plate files will be released when the units have been shipped out.

Through-hole 5x12 ortholinear PCB for 60% tray mount case

Hello Geekhack,

The Lumberjack is back for a round 2. The open source, through hole, built it yourself, 60% ortholinear PCB kit is back.

* Ortholinear PCB for 60% cases
* Designed to be easy to build with minimal soldering experience.
* All through-hole components, no SMD soldering required.
* Get that visible component aesthetic in a standard 60% case.
* 5x12 (split Preonic) layout, full grid or 2x2u spacebars.
* Supports both USB Mini and USB-C (both connectors in kit).
* Supports MX, Choc v1, and Alps switches.
* The shape of the PCB will have cutouts to make it Bakeneko 60 compatible (no JST socket in kit).
* FR4 switch plate (black).
* €22EUR + shipping from EU



More info: https://github.com/peej/lumberjack-keyboard
Geekhack IC thread: https://geekhack.org/index.php?topic=115664.0
Group Buy form: https://forms.gle/7LjG9hPLcS16DDDS9

Group Buy details:

* Group Buy open until Saturday 19th Feb
* Paypal invoices will be sent after that date
* Kit production time expected to take 6 weeks after all invoices are paid
* Choice of 7 PCB colours
* Microcontroller will be pre-flashed by myself with a bootloader and VIA compatible QMK firmware
* Kits will be shipped in a padded envelope by priority mail without tracking via PostNL from The Netherlands
* Kits will be shipped in small batches as they are completed in the order that invoices are paid
* Delivery times are expected to be long due to Covid
* Detailed build guide: https://github.com/peej/lumberjack-keyboard/blob/master/guide.md
* All new wood themed soldermask on the underside



Group Buy closed, thanks for your support

Thanks for your support!
Any questions, drop 'em below.

Far as I can tell, it’s just because tray mount is the ‘standard’ 60% with Poker screw holes. Since ortho is a niche and some people just want to try it out, being able to pop the PCB in a Tofu or similar starter case without committing is a nice option. That's why you see so few group buys for high end ortho keyboards, for example gaskets, as very few people are willing to go outside their preferences.

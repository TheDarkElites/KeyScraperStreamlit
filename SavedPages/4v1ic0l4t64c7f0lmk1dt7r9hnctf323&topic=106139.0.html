Austin R2 - A compact keyboard for your full size needs.


Specs
  • Full aluminum top and bottom case
  • 6.5 degree typing angle
  • Sandblasted raw brass weight
  • Sandblasted raw brass plate
  • PCB is QMK/VIA compatible
  • USB C
  • Top mounted

For more information about the board, please check out https://driftmechanics.com/pages/austin-info


Color Options
  • E-White
  • Charcoal Gray
  • Cabernet Red

Price
  • Base kit (includes brass plate, PCB, fasteners, bumpons) - $525
  • Extra PCB (Limit 2) - $60 (compatible with R1/R2)
  • Extra Plate (Limit 2) Brass, Polycarb, or Carbon Fiber - $45 (Only compatible with R2)

Layout


Where to purchase

US/CAN Buyers - US/CAN buyers will be able to purchase on https://www.driftmechanics.com. These orders will only be available for US/CAN buyers and products will be available at 10AM CT on May 16th. There will be 75 units, FCFS. Extra PCBs and plates will be available 30 mins before so you can add them to your cart ahead of time.


International Buyers - Anyone outside of the US will be able to purchase through ilumkb (https://www.ilumkb.com). A product page will be available the day before and a google form will be in the description. GB starts at 3PM SGT on May 16th. Hopefully this time should work for those in EU and Asia. There will be 75 units, FCFS. ISO plate options should be available on the form.

For those who might not be able to join during the FCFS, there will be extras available after the GB units are delivered. Please do not ask until then.


Leadtime
5-6 months


Disclaimer on colors
Many of you have asked about the Charcoal gray. The chip that was provided to me doesn’t seem to match the prototype received by ilumkb so I will defer to his pictures for a more accurate color representation of the Charcoal gray. There is no guarantee that the production colors will be a 100% match to the prototype but should be very close. The manufacturer will generally match up to 90% of the pantone color and because anodization happens in batches, it is possible that not all grays or reds will turn out exactly the same. Please understand that there can be a lot of variances when it comes to which monitor you use to see the pictures or videos as well. The goal for the Charcoal gray is to be darker than the R1’s gray but not as dark as black.


Quality disclosure
A-stock is considered a board that does not have visible marks on the exterior parts of the board when the case is assembled. Small anodization variations might be visible in harsh lighting. Our goal is to order enough extras so that each GB participant should receive an A-stock board.


Updates
Updates will be provided whenever they are available. Normally during the first month or two during production, there will not be any updates besides the boards being in production. Once we are getting closer to the end of the lead time, I will provide as many updates as the manufacturer will provide me. I will update the GB post for all relevant updates               


Additional pictures









For those who missed the stream, please check out the VOD for the changes between R1 and R2. https://www.twitch.tv/videos/608622670

IC Thread

Differences between R1 and R2
  • 6.5 degree angle in R2 vs 5.5 degree angle in R1
  • Small plate changes to improve sound
  • Brass weight does not cut into the case anymore
  • Cavity between PCB and bottom piece has been reduced
  • Different bumpons

Polycarb Austin Auction

To celebrate the start of R2, I will be auctioning a 1 of 1 production polycarbonate Austin based on the R1 design. The google form will open up on the 9th and will close Monday morning (whenever I wake up) on the 11th. This polycarb case is the only one of its kind since it was based on the production files from R1 and not the prototype. The case and weight will be sold “as-is” since there are no extras to replace it. 5/16/20 - Orders have been closed! I would say it was a pretty successful ordering day! Each group sold out of their 75 units in a minute or less. Thank you everyone for your support to make this GB takes its first step into becoming a reality. I apologize for those who had any issues or wasn't able to grab one. There will be extras so please keep an eye out once all the GB boards have been delivered. Currently we are tallying up all of the orders and will work on placing our orders with the various vendors. Once all orders have been placed, it becomes the waiting game. I plan to update the discord and this post for any updates. However, please expect not much to happen within the first 2-3 months. Thanks!

5/21/20 - Order has been placed with the case manu and payments from both vendors have been made so they can arrange for production now. woohoo

6/19/20 - Manufacturer is finishing up the testing of the first board with all the screws and a slight revision was approved. Everything seems to be going well, no issues so far from the manufacturer.

7/14/20 - Boards are currently anodizing. Boxes and screws have been ordered. PCBs are still in manufacturing stage. All other accessories are already at the vendors.

8/6/20 - Boards are starting to be assembled and packed up. PCBs are stuck in customs and I need to dispute tariffs. Everything still on schedule so far.

8/13/20 - Austins are finally in assembly. I was told they were in assembly last week but the brass weights still needed sand blasting. With regards to PCBs. they're in my city but DHL still hasn't released them even though I've paid the duty on them. One of the packages are also "delivered" even though I do not have them but there will be a DHL agent contacting me tomorrow about what needs to be done. I don't doubt that I'll receive 2/3 pieces but I hope they actually have the 3rd piece and just scanned it wrong. Due to the current situation, the case manu will be shipping in batches since they're only allowed to ship 100kg each day. They also need to order stronger cartons to ship them so hopefully it'll only delay us maybe a week or so. A lot of these things are out of our control but rest assured, I will continue to work behind the scenes to try to get the Austin out as quickly as I can. Based on the current timeline, I still think we're doing really well so please be patient . Please check the discord for pictures. 

8/20/20 - So I just got off the phone with DHL. It's been 7 days since the inquiry was opened and they're basically saying that the two PCB packages are lost. They're thinking it was mis-sorted and went somewhere else. I've emailed the PCB manu and am asking them to start working on making more PCBs as soon as possible. I'm not sure how they're going to handle it but I'll see what they can do. Currently I have 40 pieces so probably the first 20-30 or so orders shouldn't be delayed (depending on how many extra PCBs were ordered). Sorry for the bad news but sometimes these things happen. Hopefully my next update will be more exciting.

8/26/20 - Boards have been picked up by the shipping company and should take 10-15 days to get to me. The PCB manufacturer has agreed to remake the PCBs and should be able to ship them out to be by the middle of September. Hopefully if everything runs smoothly from here on out, we should be able to still get boards out according to schedule.

9/10/20 - Boards have been received! I'm waiting for one last box of cases that hopefully should arrive today or tomorrow. PCB manufacturer said the PCBs should be ready to be shipped early next week. I've started shipping some of the boards and will continue to work on QC'ing/shipping in batches. The boards weren't packaged in the best manner so some of the packaging boxes were damaged and/or dirty. In order to not cause any delays, I'll be using the packaging boxes that are still intact and will order new packaging boxes for the extras. Also, as a reminder, A-stock is considered a board that does not have visible marks on the exterior parts of the board when the case is assembled. However, due to how e-white is finished, there might be small specks caused from dust while the e-white was curing.

9/23/20 - PCBs have been received! I'll start shipping out batches tomorrow! I hope to get out everything by the end of the week.

10/18/20 - I believe all units have been shipped or packaged to be shipped now. Extras raffle will be announced later this month. I'm still waiting for packaging before I can ship them. Thanks!

I appreciate everyone's passion for this board. I just wanted to clear things up since it seems like there are some assumptions that I don't believe to be true.

The limit of 75 boards for both US and Int'l is not to keep the board exclusive. If you compare this round to round 1, we are delivering 4x more boards in total once GB and extras are done. If I am trying to make an exclusive board, I would have kept it the same number or less than R1.

Most GBs run by GB runner not sponsored by a full time vendor are limited groupbuys. Novelkeys has been around for a few years now and have enough manpower and capital to run an unlimited GB. Even if I did have more money, that does not reduce the amount of effort it takes to run a GB, not to mention my full time job that I still have.

As a one man crew, I am only comfortable handling up to 75 boards at this moment in time. If I become more efficient and adding more boards would not cause the quality to lower, I might consider doing more at that time.

For those who think running unlimited groupbuys means that the cost of materials essentially become zero, I would like to disagree. The design changes for this round actually made cost of materials higher than round 1 even with the increase in units. However, I felt that the changes were beneficial and made the decision to take that risk. So even though I am ordering what I consider to be a lot more than the first round, the costs from the manufacturer is more per unit than what I paid the first round. I can only do this because of the faith and support I had from R1 participants.

Thanks for your understanding and patience. I'm trying to push as many boards as I can to whoever is interested. I'm really grateful there are so many people interested. Hopefully I can strive to do more in future rounds.

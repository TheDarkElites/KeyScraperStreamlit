FYI a couple details that I just want to point out about the keyboard

1) note that the case is technically multiple pieces but it functions as a one-piece case - this means the plate/pcb assembly drops into the case from the top. The newer duck builds have the plate screwed onto the bottom of the top piece. If you're not careful putting the pcb/plate into the case, you can scratch it.
2) the plate supports switch top removal in just about every position UNLESS you use some of the non-standard layouts. I have my bottom row set up as 1.25 1.25 1.25 6.25 1.5 1.5 1 1 1 and some of those keys, plus some of the numpad keys, don't allow the top to be pulled out.
3) the gold spacebar stabilizer is a little shorter than the ones I got from originative and I think it makes the spacebar rattle a little bit, since it's loose horizontally.
4) the "holes" on the sides of the triangular cutouts are spaced where there are RGB LEDs, but since they're below the frosted diffuser, don't actually shine light like on duck builds. It's mostly a cosmetic thing. Unless you had a triangular diffuser cut, and I still don't think it would shine like on a duck.

minor details, but these are things that I wouldn't have considered without having spent some time with the board.

Yeah, I've noticed that they appear more orange than the SPRiT stabs I've used before.

FYI a couple details that I just want to point out about the keyboard

1) note that the case is technically multiple pieces but it functions as a one-piece case - this means the plate/pcb assembly drops into the case from the top. The newer duck builds have the plate screwed onto the bottom of the top piece. If you're not careful putting the pcb/plate into the case, you can scratch it.
2) the plate supports switch top removal in just about every position UNLESS you use some of the non-standard layouts. I have my bottom row set up as 1.25 1.25 1.25 6.25 1.5 1.5 1 1 1 and some of those keys, plus some of the numpad keys, don't allow the top to be pulled out.
3) the gold spacebar stabilizer is a little shorter than the ones I got from originative and I think it makes the spacebar rattle a little bit, since it's loose horizontally.
4) the "holes" on the sides of the triangular cutouts are spaced where there are RGB LEDs, but since they're below the frosted diffuser, don't actually shine light like on duck builds. It's mostly a cosmetic thing. Unless you had a triangular diffuser cut, and I still don't think it would shine like on a duck.

minor details, but these are things that I wouldn't have considered without having spent some time with the board.

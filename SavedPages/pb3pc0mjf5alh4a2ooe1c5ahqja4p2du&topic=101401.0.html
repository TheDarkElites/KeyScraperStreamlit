JER PLANT - J80 (JER-A06) Round 2 "Electrophoresis White"
The J80 is a OG Cherry style(no plate) custom 80% kit.
It is characterized by the fact that the hoisting PCB has no steel plate and can fully reflect the elasticity of the linear shaft. Tested by the buyer, its feel and typing sound are different from other custom keyboards and are satisfactory. It has a simple and elegant original cherry-like shape, the author's original intention is to start from the touching feel, let everyone have a comfortable tapping experience. The light stick module in the upper right corner is detachable, it could be switches (3 keys) or the light stick module. When using light stick module, PrtScr Scrolllock and Pause are achieved by pressing FN+ Insert / Home / Page Up.

However, due to the tight schedule and the number of orders in China at that time, in order to ensure the quality, 40 sets were limited. On the same day, the group was cut off. Later, due to the author's personal PAYPAL problem, a small part of the order was cut, and I would like to say sorry to all the people who has any trouble with J80 R1 group buy.

At the end of this month, we will bring you a new surface treatment process of J80 - electrophoresis white and anodized J80.
Electrophoresis is characterized by a delicate surface and durability, and this time the electrophoresis white is the same as the white craft used in the previous JER MINI, but there is still a little difference. In order to make it look more retro, the factory opened a brand new processing pool - it will be a slightly yellowish milky white color, compared to the micro-arc gray of j80 R1, both the texture and the color itself have a great upgrade,  check the following pictures for a compariation of the micro-arc gray in R1 and the new electro white in R2.

For Round 2 there're 3 colors to choose from: electrophoresis white, black (anodized), dark gray (anodized). Due to cost, electrophoresis white will be slightly more expensive. This group will no longer provide J PAD or provide J80 NCR color matching, because the author is trying to do some new process J80 NCR color matching which takes some time.

Introduction:
Got hook into this hobby from the sight of DanGWanG's beautiful KMAC. Back then, KMAC came in like a wrecking ball, wrecking many, many wallets, and that kick start the Korean custom keyboard hype from that day onwards. Having tasted the many Korean custom boards, the ideas start pouring in, and in that mix, the best quality ingredients (or features, in this case) are picked up and thrown into the design. With the help of a group of enthusiasts with similar interest, the design is polished from time to time, prototypes after prototypes, until the satisfactory result is achieved. The TGR is ready.

“Everything should be made as simple as possible, but no simpler.” – Albert Einstein.

This is what TGR Jane is all about, a quality, clean, solid, elegant custom keyboard, no fancy pancy, only classy. The beauty of it is astounding despite its plain Jane design. But lo and behold, the value of it lies within, as all of the built focus is in making sure the keyboard delivers a solid feel to the one who wields it.

  • Full aluminum case
  • 8 degrees slope
  • Brass weight
  • Plate material: stainless steel, aluminum, acrylic

  • Fully Programmable
  • Full LED backlighting
  • Controller parts are pre-soldered

Full plate is just as it sounds.  Half plate covers only the mods, not the alphas (see photo below from Reaper).



Many claim that certain switch types feel superior without a plate, but the modifiers might feel better with the plate, hence the half plate.  It's something that many might consider unnecessary, and is more prominent in the Korean community.

Quote from: tlt on Tue, 28 July 2015, 13:47:17
Can someone explain the difference between full plate and half plate
Full plate is like most modern boards, fully plate mounted across the board. Half plate is where the modifiers are plate mounted,  but with a large cutout in the plate for the alpha keys which are pcb mounted. I've only seen this done with a Korean custom board, the Cheat IIRC. It's done for the reasons Hoff said plus with the alphas being pcb mounted you can easily switch out spring/stems which can be nice.

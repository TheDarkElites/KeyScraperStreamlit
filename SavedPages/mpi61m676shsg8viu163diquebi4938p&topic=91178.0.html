Shipping complete. This group buy is done.


This is a Group Buy for the Kona Classic, a 60% layout + Left F-Keys.

Prototype Photo


Production Updates:
More
Sept 7: All invoices have been sent out. Parts that have the longest lead times will be ordered tonight. RGB LED Strips are already on the way.

Sept 16: LED Strips received.
Sept 21: All case parts received. PCBs should arrive on the 25th.
Sept 25: PCBs received. They will be tested and a build guide will be created in the next few days. Shipping is on track for this weekend (Sept 30th)
Sept 26: Initial PCB test was a success! ISO test required, and then packing will begin!



I set up a GitHub repo to host assembly info and the Firmware: https://github.com/DangerousParts/KonaClassic

Build Log


Supported Layouts:

  • Left Side F-Keys
  • ANSI & ISO Support
  • Arrow Cluster / Short right shift options
  • Split Spacebar

Ordering closed

Specifications:
  • Acrylic + Delrin Sandwich Case
  • MX Compatible PCB (Sorry Alps users)
  • Through-hole diodes
  • RGB Underglow supported (WS2812B LED Strips)
  • Teensy 2.0 Controlled
  • Green PCB
  • QMK Firmware (configuration to be posted on GitHub)
  • Does not support backlighting

Parts needed to build your own Kona Classic:
  • Kona Classic PCB
  • 72-76 1N4148 Diodes
  • 72-76 MX compatible switches & Stabilizers (not included in this GB)
  • Teensy 2.0
  • Case & Fasteners
  • Optional RGB LED Underglow Strip

Pricing:
  • Full Kit: $90
  • Individual Parts:
    • PCB: $15
    • Acrylic + Delrin Sandwich Case (includes fasteners): $50
    • 80 1N4148 Diodes: $2
    • Teensy 2.0 (includes headers): $18
    • WS2812B LED Strip (1 meter / 30 LEDs): $5

All parts can be purchased individually. There is no MOQ for any of the parts.

Shipping:
  • United States (Including AK & HI): $15 + $7 per additional kit or case
  • International: $35 + $15 per additional kit or case

Additional Information:
All PayPal fees are included in the Group Buy pricing.

PCB dimensional schematic: https://i.imgur.com/kbSh4OR.png

Sandwich Layers SVG: download

Firmware will be posted in the official QMK GitHub repository before production ends. Waiting on approval.

If you purchase a kit or an extra case, you will be contacted during the payment phase to select your case colors.
  • Switch plate will always be black
  • Middle layers can be a combination of black, white, or clear (other colors may be available)
  • Bottom plate can be black or clear

All prices are in USD and include PayPal fees. Prices do not include shipping. PayPal is the only way to pay for this Group Buy.

Parts can be purchased as a complete kit or individually.

All parts are through-hole (diodes, Teensy, switches), so soldering should be easy for everyone. There is no assembly service for this Group Buy.

  • Production: September 11 - 29
  • Shipping Complete within 2 weeks of production completion
  • GB Complete <--- We are here

Quote from: Philrulesworld on Mon, 04 September 2017, 16:53:59
How complicated is soldering the teensy on this? Do the solder joints have to be flat like on a Phantom PCB build?

On a semi-related note, will there be some sort of build log posted by the time this ships?

Since it's all through-hole soldering, soldering the parts (teensy & diodes) should be simple. It's the same as soldering switches.

The Teensy headers don't stick up further than the USB port on the teensy, so you don't need to clip them. The only thing that needs to be clipped are the diode legs once they're soldered in.

I'll make a build log with detailed photos (since I'll have to build one more board when the GB PCBs arrive to be 1 million percent sure they're perfect) and am including a short instructional sheet with all orders noting the correct build order (diodes -> stabs -> teensy headers -> switches -> teensy).

GMK LAZURITE
An allusion to High Renaissance art and the value of the pigment ultramarine

GMK Lazurite takes inspiration from the mineral lazurite found in lapis lazuli, processed and turned into a pigment called ultramarine. It was used by High Renaissance painters for the color blue, and was even priced more than the value of gold.

High Renaissance - Novelties Kits 1 and 2 ($60/kit)
The idea is to create a paint frame design for your mechanical keyboards using the novelties, which will act as 'borders' on ortholinears, usual 40% layouts, 60%, 65%, 75%, 80%/TKL, Tsangan, HHKB, WKL, 1800 and full-sized keyboards. (almost all layouts are supported by both novelties kits)

Hi loop. Thank you for taking the time to leave your feedback about Lazurite!

Yes I get a lot of feedback/criticisms (maybe) for having a lot of collaborations. Majority of these collabs (especially artisan keycaps) are fairly new in the hobby and I believe I can be a bridge for them to have another platform to share their amazing craft. Of course it is mutually beneficial for the keyset and the collaborator; and thats how it should be. By doing this, I was also able to gain a lot of friends in them along the way and get to learn more of the work poured into their craft.

Another consideration I had is the frustration of GB participants who buy a keyset and want a themed collaboration artisan with a very slim chance of winning raffle sales. Its been a very tough project management for me but rest assured I make sure to not miss in promoting every collab in all platforms where Lazurite is uploaded. The design I envisioned was for the community after all and thats how its supposed to be.

Im just happy to get more involved in this hobby and to share my passion with the community. Thanks loop!

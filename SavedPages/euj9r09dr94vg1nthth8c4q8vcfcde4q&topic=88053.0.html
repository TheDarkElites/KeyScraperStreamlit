Personally, I love high gloss mirror like finish, I use the following steps for most of my project finishes:

Apply appropriate Filler (normally I work a lot with Open Grain wood like Walnut, so I tend to stick with water based filler)
scrape off excess after dry
use burlap to remove rest of excess until all filler is removed
220 grit light sanding
apply sealer coat of lacquer
scuff sanding with 320 grit
8 coats of lacquer then let dry for 8-10 days
Level finish wet sand using dish soap and water and 400 grit

Quote from: FoC_Tow on Fri, 24 March 2017, 15:19:29
In the sample pics black walnut looks fully gray to me, but finishing seems to add a brownish tint to it.

Is the tint really a result of the finish?

Yes, you're exactly right - the raw wood looks flat gray, and any finish will amplify more of the underlying color, no matter the wood. Oil-based finishes tend to penetrate deeper, and make the color deeper / warmer (which is usually exactly what you want). 

Quote from: FoC_Tow on Fri, 24 March 2017, 15:19:29
Would I be able to get a raw black walnut, and would adding s clear finish maintain the gray color?

A water-based finish is an option - these don't penetrate as much and affect the color less, though it still won't be grey. Stained oak could be a possibility also.

Hi, thankyou for the kind words, we are glad you love the design I understand your concerns. The "MACRO-1" is quite a large macro pad, the case weights in at ~700g, compare this to our "Raindrop 60%" weighing in at ~950g. The manufacturing cost of low quantity production items lays mostly outside the actual material cost, as such size isn't a huge factor in cost. This alongside the falling USD and sky rocketing raw material and electrical component costs is really hurting the custom keeb scene at the moment. You can see more discussion about this on the IC https://geekhack.org/index.php?topic=110130.msg2990781#msg2990781

We did poll this to the community, and with over 1000 responses, the overwhelming majority viewed the price point as fair. If you have any other questions please feel free to ask

We had a similar query during the IC. To us, it felt more natural and ergonomic to have the knob on the left and control the encoder with your index and thumb whilst supporting your hand with your pinky and ring finger on the sides of the case. Looking at other projects, I would say at a rough estimate, 70% of macropads have followed this same logic. The feedback that we received from the testers is that the encoder feels comfortable and natural in its current position

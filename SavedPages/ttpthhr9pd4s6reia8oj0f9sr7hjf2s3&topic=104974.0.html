Drink Me is a 4% ergo 3D-printed macropad.

The project started as a taster for friends who are getting interested in mechanical keyboards; something inexpensive that would allow them to experiment with customising keymaps, try out different keycaps and switches (its hotswap), and take it apart to see how the components fit together.

The design is a lighthearted homage to the many semi-angled ergonomic keyboard designs emerging in the community in recent times, such as the Lyn EM.7, TGR Alice, and Prime_e. I was really fascinated how the design decision of rotating the inner sections of keys made such a bold statement, and was curious to see what this gesture would look like in its absolute minimal case, so here it is: 4% ergo

The goal was to produce a pleasing artefact that celebrates a popular form, while being capable of all the programmability and customization that excite us as enthusiasts, and staying accessible to newcomers. Drink Me is a fun and functional macropad; an ideal gift or conversation piece for coaxing friends, family and coworkers to follow you down the rabbit hole.

Following a successful interest check, I am pleased to announce the group buy for this project will open on March 5th 2020.

Events

  • 2020-03-09 - The USB-C connector (GCT USB4105) that the prototype boards were designed around has suddenly become unavailable. I spent some time on the phone to the manufacturer and distributors in Europe and the United States today and was told that the earliest it will become available again for retail is July 4th 2020. That is clearly not an acceptable delay so I have rapidly redesigned the board around the popular HRO TYPE-C-31-M-12 connector. The connector components and revised prototype PCBs have already been ordered and should arrive within 10 days.
  • 2020-03-13 - The Spanish government has announced a state of emergency due to COVID-19. Courier deliveries are arriving but deliveries by national mail have not been operating in my area in recent days. No outgoing deliveries will be possible during the current rules which will last at least 15 days. Production remains on track.
  • 2020-03-20 - Replacement USB-C connectors and PCB have arrived. They have passed all tests and final USB-C PCBs have been ordered. All other PCBs and most other electronic components have also been received.
  • 2020-03-22 - The state of emergency in Spain has been extended until at least the 12th of April. My orders of switches, keycaps and hotswap sockets will probably be delayed beyond their initial expected delivery dates. Production of printed parts continues unaffected, though I will not be able to ship any completed units while the state of emergency continues in place.
  • 2020-03-29 - The Spanish government has ruled that only essential activities will be permitted between March 30th and April 9th. It is likely that deliveries of PCBs, filament, switches and hotswap sockets will not reach me during this time. Production continues to schedule but delays are possible.
  • 2020-04-13 - Restrictions on movement continue in Spain, until April 24th at least, so it is still not possible to mail any completed units. Today some sectors were allowed to return to work, and it is hoped that the Kailh hotswap sockets will arrive as delivery services resume operations. Production of printed parts has be temporarily paused as the printer has been in full time use producing face masks for frontline healthcare workers in response to the ongoing health crisis.
  • 2020-04-13 - Hotswap sockets have still not arrived. I have taken the step of ordering another replacement batch from a different supplier with express shipping.
  • 2020-04-24 - Restrictions on movement here in Spain have been extended until at least the 9th of May, so no completed units can be shipped before then. The replacement order of hotswap sockets is programmed for delivery on the 27th of April, with which assembly of PCBs can continue. Printing continues. Blue, red and magenta units are mostly complete and translucent pearl parts are currently in progress.
  • 2020-04-27 - Hotswap sockets have arrived. Pearl printed parts are nearly complete.
  • 2020-05-11 - The Spanish postal service has published a list of countries with currently viable operating routes. This does not currently cover all Drink Me buyers.
  • 2020-06-05 - Exactly 3 months after opening the GB, all units have been produced and passed quality control. All units with viable shipping routes have been shipped. 12% are currently unable to ship but I am checking regularly.
  • 2020-07-10 - New Zealand and Singapore have begun accepting mail from Spain again, so these orders have been shipped. Australia and Faroe Isles are still not accepting mail from here, but I continue to check daily.
  • 2020-08-10 - The Faroe Isles have begun accepting mail from Spain again, so these orders have been shipped. Australia is still not accepting mail from here, but I continue to check daily.
  • 2020-10-12 - Remaining buyers and giveaway winner in Australia have been contacted individually to discuss compensation options since shipping routes look unlikely to reopen soon.

Quote
Awesome! Out of curiosity, can I ask what printer you have / are using? I've been thinking about getting into printing.

I bought a Prusa i3 clone a couple of years ago and have been gradually and persistently replacing every part of it for higher quality components ever since. I love it, and I've learnt a lot from the experience, but it has not been the most efficient or economical route to getting a nice printer! My advice would be to simply get a Prusa MK3S; everyone I know that has one is extremely happy with it.

Whatever printer you buy, some tips:

  • Learn how to level your build surface precisely
  • Learn how to get really good first-layer adhesion (generally by printing a bit hotter and slower than the rest)
  • Budget some time for regular printer maintenance
  • Find some calibration models that you like
  • Treat every new filament as uncharted territory and spend some time calibrating it when you first load it
  • Consider planning to keep your printer in an enclosure. This helps a lot to avoid pieces warping, but it may require extra cooling for your electronics.

I'm excited to announce that the ErgoDox Wireless group buy sale has started! We've taken the ErgoDox and redesigned it to be completely wireless. No wire between the two halves, no wire to your computer, and you can easily switch between multiple devices wirelessly.

  • The group buy will run until March 7, 2021.
  • Group buy orders with the laser cut case is estimated to ship in May and June. Orders with the aluminum CNC case is estimated to ship in June and July.
  • The kit starts at $222, which gets you PCBs ($120), layered laser cut case without solar ($90), and batteries ($12).

Main Product Page



Key Features
  • Bluetooth Low Energy wireless
  • Nordic nRF52840 SoC with 1MB flash and 256KB memory
  • USB C
  • LiPoly battery charger
  • Preinstalled Kailh hot swap sockets (optional addon)
  • Solar charging (optional addon)
  • Extended battery life (around a year per charge with a dongle, even longer with solar charging)
  • ZMK firmware support

Product Links
  • Layered Laser Cut Case
  • Aluminum CNC Case
  • Travel Carrying Case
  • Tenting Kit

This is a project that started in 2019 and was followed up with a redesign in 2020 that included major enhancements. The group buy version is a further improvement on the 2020 redesign as the assembly process has been greatly simplified. See the assembly page for more information. An assembly service is also offered for those that don't want to do any soldering/assembly on their own.

If you have any questions or concerns about the project or group buy, please do not hesitate to reach out. You can reach me here on Geekhack, Discord, or via the store's contact form.

Quote from: roostrc0gburn on Sat, 20 February 2021, 14:43:26
any plans for a tented base?

Yes, definitely planned. There's a placeholder product page at https://www.slicemk.com/products/ergodox-wireless-tenting-kit. Most people use tenting to reduce wrist pronation and the plate helps do that. A couple of screws (with nuts underneath) are used to adjust the height. It'll be M5 or 10-32 screws so it should be fairly stable. The head can have a rubber feet to prevent scratching the table. The other holes are for the keyboard screws and keyboard rubber feet to sit in so the keyboard doesn't slide sideways.

I opted for this design because you can remove the keyboard or disassemble the entire tenting kit very quickly. It's also cheap/replaceable components and easy to extend if you want to do something crazier (like tenting the keyboard vertically).

I just ordered the actual prototype a couple of days ago. Once I have it (in a week or so) I'll definitely share photos here. Below you can see a quick cardboard prototype that I put together to demonstrate how it would work.

    For those who don't know the character on the Rama artisan, I just wanted to let you know how amazing this really is. You could argue that it is one of the most fundamental and important characters of Asian history and philosophy. 
       

    The history behind this character is very long and complicated, and there is so much to go into that you could keep reading books about it's meaning for years.   
    It originates (maybe) from the Dao De Jing by Lao-Tzu, and represents an interpretation of the dao, the underlying force that controls and orders everything in the physical and energetic universe. You could compare it to what the greeks called Logos, and in India it's known as Dharma. It is the basic idea in daoism, a philosophy and religion with the objective to live in harmony with the dao. 

    It's a hugely important character that has shaped and influenced many of the great eastern philosophies we know today, and I was so happy to see it find its way to mechanical keyboard community

PHANTOM PLATES AND LINERS



Phantom options leave a bit to be desired these days, what with the progression of design over these few years. I've slowly been putting together things to help with this, if only a small amount. The easiest and most affordable starting point seems to be plates, and FR4 is a perfect option for test runs. In the future other material options may be available, but that depends on interest and successfulness of projects.

This will be a small test run to see how well the design works, and iron out small kinks. This will currently only be the plates + liners and not any PCBs, but I am in works with a few people to design better PCBs for filco/phantom style cases that should be affordable, and up to date with current standards.





GROUP BUY FORM

Details and Notes
  • I'm currently only doing fixed ansi in this test run, but am open to universal or fixed ISO in the future.
  • The design is inspired by OTD plates, relief cuts are fun!
  • Compatible with cherry stabalizers as well as costar for this run, in the future these will be offered with a PCB and will only support PCB mount cherry stabs.
  • This will technically work with the stock PCB of a Filco with a small modification(see below*), BUT I do recommend an aftermarket PCB solution such as a Phantom or an LFK-TKL. And if you're patient, I will be running a buy with more affordable PCBs in the future.
  • 1.6mm thick, for now

OPTIONS

Plates
  • FR4 Full plate - $20
  • FR4 Half plate - $20
  • Micarta** Full plate (Limit of 5) - $25
Liners
  • Neoprene Firm - Good old neoprene. This should work well to reduce vibration, and should dampen the sound of the board a bit. This would be a middle of the road option, with some benefits from each of the ones below.
  • Cork Rubber - - This is a cork and neoprene blend with medium density, high durability, and decent sound dampening. This will dampen the sound similar to the full neoprene, but should have a deeper sound signature, lighter weight, and will allow for more plate flex. I'm personally partial to this, because it looks cool as hell, and I like the idea of a deeper more "woody" sound.
  • Closed Cell Sponge Rubber - This is essentially high density neoprene foam. Because this is a foam, it will almost definitely provide the most sound dampening. Normally the space between the plate and the PCB is 1/8" inch, and I can get this at 3/16", since this is a foam like material it will compress and reduce vibration/sound a bit more.
  • All Material Options Respectively - $22 per liner
Shipping
  • $12 CONUS, Individually determined international ***

    Disclaimer:
    I want to emphasize again that this is a test run. An initial effort to work out issues users may have when using the plates, so that future iterations will be tested, tried, and true. While I've had assistance from other users to make sure all is well, I cannot in good faith guarantee that the plates will fit all switches/stabs/PCBs/etc. in all instances. What I can say is that the file sent to manu is built with the open source phantom plate as a base. I've edited around the already existing plate files to ensure that the majority of elements on the plate are directly transferred from an existing drawing that has been tested many many times.

    I will be building a test board after receiving the plates, before sending out orders, to be sure that there are no issues. If after receiving plates there is a glaring issue somehow overlooked by me, I will do my best to replace plates for users out of pocket, and only charge shipping to send a replacement out.

    Annotations
    * Modifying the plate to work with the stock PCB is quite easy, since FR4 is relatively easy to cut, even with a hobby knife. The picture below shows where you will need to cut to accommodate for the PCB controller. The integrity of the plate will not be compromised from this cut.




    **  I have been informed that the Micarta would present some issues with cutting so thin, potential shattering, and a special way that it needs to be cut would raise the price significantly, close to $40 a plate NOT including material. In the future we may try true Micarta, but for now they've presented me with an alternative material that will be very inexpensive and has most of the same properties, just is not laminated after finishing. It's a polyester based "plastic" made in the same way as Micarta (layered, heated, then pressed), with a different resin used as the binding agent, typically used as shim stock. I will start an IC eventually to see if people are willing to pay the premium to have true Micarta cut in the future, possibly pairing with a carbon fiber option.


    *** International shipping is looking to be slightly inflated if you are only purchasing one item. Based on estimates so far, the total will be betweeen $20 and $35
    If this gets too expensive and enough people order overseas then I'll be looking into a proxy to reduce shipping cost to you guys.

    Ok, All materials are in hand and I'll be shipping out orders very soon, so be on the lookout for shipping invoices! Once these are out and paid, I'll be able to order proper shipping materials to prevent bending and breakage.

    I'm currently waiting on labels to get here for my new shipping label printer, and I'm also shipping out a small package for a test build just to be sure. I would wait on the test build to happen, but honestly I've made you all wait long enough.

    I'll post a few pictures of the liners tonight to show them off!

    One thing to note, the cork rubber neoprene and the Neoprene Firm are a bit more firm than originally anticipated. This could be a good or bad thing as I see it now. Once I've gotten my own board properly desoldered by a friend, and built with a cork rubber liner, I will upload a sound test. 

    That said, the Closed Cell Sponge Rubber turned out better than originally hoped. The cell size is a little larger than anticipated, but so far that seems like a good thing.

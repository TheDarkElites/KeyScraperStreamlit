Two different things.

There are LED holtiles, that you install into your switches, and then they are soldered into your board with the switches. That allows you to swap LEDs in the switches themselves.

Then there are switch holtites that go in the PCB and are used for switches.

Another thing I think is worth mentioning is that the gold holtites won't actually bond the same way the tin ones do. From my understanding, with tin if you use a soldering iron they actually bond to the solder pads and will stay stuck in.

I did something similar when i added holtites to my zeal60. I put a tiny bit of solder on the hole, then actually heated the holtite to reflow the solder and push the thing in. Easiest way to avoid getting solder inside the holtite that I've found.

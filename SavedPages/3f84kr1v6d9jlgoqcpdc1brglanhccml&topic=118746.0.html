”sperno 59 | 31/10/2022:"

sorry about delaying it by a week!
There was some last second logistical stuff that was overlooked for launching the GB pages, and we wanted to take a few days to iron it out.

Introduction

I don’t use right shift, I do use arrows. So why don’t replace the right shift with arrows?
This way we can keep the "60%" format, with less compromise. (unless you use right shift of course)
Sperno means to despise/remove/reject etc. in this case, the right shift is the object... Also, it sounds funny.

We have made 2 versions, one with an aluminum bottom and another with a brass bottom.
The brass bottom is heavier and sounds better than the aluminum without foam.
With the alu bottom, you depend more on the provided foam to prevent sound from bouncing inside the case.
The upside is that the aluminum version is cheaper, and maybe you prefer the full alu look.

(There is a sound test and the full alu without foam sounds really good, but decide for yourself)

Specifications

Typing angle: 6o
Dimensions: Length: 306.75 mm, Width: 118.01 mm, Front Height: 19.25 mm, back height: 28.4 mm
Case material: Aluminum (and brass)
Weight: Brass built without switches and caps:  2.25kg
            Alu built without switches and caps: 1.2kg
Colors: Anodized Black, Anodized Alu (silver), Anodized Slate Blue, Anodized Vibrant Red
Mounting: Gasket boots (similar to suit)
               The boots slide over the plate.
Plate: Polycarbonate, alu or FR4 (with a lot of flex cuts)
PCB: VIA compatible

Solder layout options:                                                                   

Hotswap layout option:



Sound and Feel

brass bottom
full alu (no foam)

The board is bouncy and types soft, this is due to the big amount of flex cuts in the plate and also in the pcb.
You can adjust the amount of gaskets to influence the feel, using all the gaskets will make it stiffer and using less will increase the flex.

Prototype and Colors

R1 proto: We are very pleased with the outcome, technically almost everything works except a tolerance issue with some switches.
For the final and R2 this issue will be solved.
We have ordered a proto of each color with pics in the comments.
The red and blue are a bit lighter than what we intended and will be darker for R2 and final.

R2 proto: Have arrived! the blue color looks way better, the red is now a better shade, but still quite a bit lighter.
We will be keeping this red though, we found it looks better than what we intended. (happy little accidents)

Everything looks good and no changes are needed, we are on the final product.

Hello fellow keeb enthusiasts! Straight from the realms of my overworked, sleep deprived grad student brain, I want to finally introduce you to a keycap set I have been working on all year, called KAMistry. KAMistry aims to bring elements (pun fully intended) of chemistry (and biology) into your keeb. The four main colors of the set (teal, light blue, light grey, and dark grey) were inspired by my repeated trips to my lab’s trusty -80 freezer. Because you can’t tell me you haven’t just looked at a freezer before and thought to yourself “Damn, that’s a nice looking freezer.” Complete with a grey face and a bright blue LED screen graced with a teal bezel, this gargantuan freezing lab companion was just begging to be used as an inspiration for pieces of matte PBT plastic.

As the name implies, this set will be in KAM profile. For those that are not familiar with the KAM profile, here is a diagram for you compared to KAT and Cherry. You can compare other profiles to it as well here: https://www.keycaps.info/stack



I have done my best to ensure the colors look accurate to the Pantones. However, do keep in mind that color can vary depending on the screen that it is viewed on. The best way to see what a color will look like as the end product is to check the pantone code in a physical guide. Samples will be ordered for comparison to those pantones!

Digital Carpentry wrist rest collab!
KAMistry has teamed up with the amazing Digital Carpentry to design a professional, wooden, science-themed wrist rest. Samples are below! I decided to go with the benzene ring icon for mine, but you will have an option of a couple icons, including the benzene ring and atom novelty, along with an option of walnut or maple. Even cooler, you'll have options on size of the wrist rest and where you want the icon! For this sample, I wanted the hexane ring to always be visible, even when typing (top image). The wrist rest will be available soon.


The following design will be a third option available. No, it's not an actual element, but it looks darn good as one if I do say so myself.

Hey there, sorry about the lack of responsiveness. It’s my first time running a set and I definitely made some mistakes (like you said, not following the GH thread). With that being said, I want to (finally) reply to your previous comments:

The collaborations happening at the wrong time were due to very strict scheduling on the part of the collaborators. We had to decide on a time for the GB. The original agreed on time for it was March 1st of this year, but then zFrontier stopped responding because they were shut down from Covid. However, we had already agreed on a time for the collaborations with Archetype and NZ Caps. Neither could push back the sales due to some reason, and thus we were forced to postpone the GB of the keycaps, but allow the sale of the collaborations since we have no control over that.

The French kit unfortunately can’t be changed following approval from zFrontier. I had the kit designed by a well-known member of the community (won’t say who it is on here) and they were designing the kit to try to keep it the size of the other foreign kits, for reasons dealing with our lack of being able to communicate with zFrontier. I asked many people to try to get it confirmed that the kitting would work for French keyboards (it was a very last minute addition after someone, may have been you, requested the kit) and nobody had suggested any changes, so we sent the kit in for pricing to zFrontier.

Sorry again for my lack of responsiveness. I always respond immediately on discord, but Geekhack I’m still getting used to. I hope you understand!

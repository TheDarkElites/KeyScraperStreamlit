Update : 21/07/2021

Hey guys, deskmats are already there ! 





As you can see, the results are pretty good. I tried my best to capture them with natural light, very close to the original.

Since I want to be transparent with you, I have to note 2 things for the "No Signal" Deskmat.

1) Due to the color, some micro tiny blackspots can be in the deskmat itself.
It's not noticable especially once it's on your desk. It's unavoidable and not really disturbing but I wanted to point it out.

Other than that, it's close to the original render and the white/greyish tone turned out really good. Depending on your lights it can either be super white or a nice shade of grey !

2) I had to change the font that says "No Signal" due to copyright issues. This change isnt massive and isnt really affecting the design by any means. (I personally prefere this new font)

I hope that it's not a huge deal breaker for you guys.

Thanks again for your support.

Will update you guys on the samples (for the keycap set) as soon as I get them.

- Enjoy.

Hey there, sorry for the lack of news.

We had some trouble making the first samples (that should be made """"pretty soon"""") because of the shortage of pantone plastic chips. I managed to find some paper ones (with a nice coating and everything), but the person im talking to says that it dosen't work, while most people in the hobby use them.
Don't ask me why they didn't want, I can't really give you a proper answer.

We finally managed to find out of a random shop for the "plastic chips" needed to color match the set, and it should arrive in around 10 days to GMK.

It took so long because GMK basically didn't want to do any types of effort (at least the person im working/talking with) to even make some approximate samples, or even match them to the paper one that I found, ghosting me and don't replying to my email.

At the end of the day it's all resolved, I'll have the samples in hand, but I can't believe it took this long due to the attitude of someone else while other creators still use pantone paper chips to color match their sets.

Anyways, I just wanted to give some insight on what was happening behind the stage.

I can't wait to share the samples with you guys !

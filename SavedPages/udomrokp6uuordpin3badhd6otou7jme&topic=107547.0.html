Im torn on this one.  The renders look very classy.  But I have 3c accents on my board right in front of me and they just aren't that bright.  The sample cap looks right in the photo.  But the renders look too bright.  That would be a powerful light to make 3c that vibrant.

Renders generally have some slightly more intense lighting than you would find in normal everyday use. Imagine the render being set up in a photobox type situation, with lights pointing directly at your subject. Renders will always look slightly different look than your final product, as they are generally a representation of what you are purchasing. This is why the unprofessional photo of the stock color caps is also displayed.

There are some slightly outdated renders in this GMK Pono album that might also help potentially visualize how the set will look.

I faced this issue with Wasabi (also stock colors), where the green was very tough to capture, it either looked too washed out or too dark, but looks good in person.

I feel confident the set will look good in person, as stock colors get matched very well.

From my understanding, SP has raised SA prices for 2017 production queue.

The goal here was to make a base kit that would have a relatively wide coverage in terms of compatibilty. Novelty kit is a nice to have but was not the focus and has a higher cost because it's split out.

Our full kit has 156 keys and based on SA Camping's thread, they had 150 keys in the kit. Yes it's more expensive across the two, but we decided to divide up into 2 kits because we felt the DC kit would not be as much in demand and wanted to offer a lower price for a set that could cover multiple keyboard layouts ($160 at MoQ of 200 for the Base).

With the cost increases, return ISO for our European friends, and HHKB/FC660 compatibility, we do not feel this price is unfair at all, especially for the Base Kit.

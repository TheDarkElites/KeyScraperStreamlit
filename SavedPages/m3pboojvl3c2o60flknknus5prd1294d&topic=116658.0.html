-A Bit of History-

More

                                                                                                                                                                                     
Mies van der Rohe was commissioned by the German Government in 1928 to design and construct the German National Pavilion (more commonly known as the Barcelona Pavilion) for the 1929 Barcelona International Exposition. Collaborating with Lilly Reich, the pavilion was designed and built in a remarkably short amount of time- 18 months from design to unveiling. Completed in 1929, the building quickly became a Modernist classic, embodying the progressive, sleek and forward thinking ethos behind the movement. The original pavilion stood only for a short time, and was demolished in 1930 at the end of the exposition. In its short time standing, the pavilion gained an almost mystical aura, becoming one of the most influential modernist buildings to have ever been built; while hardly being seen by anyone. The pavilion was reconstructed during 1984-1986 by local Spanish architects and the Barcelona City Council on its original site, as a permanant structure. Rebuilt according to the original drawings and photographs, the Barcelona Pavilion in its current incarnation continues to be a remarkable piece of architecture that delights visitors today, and is a testament to Mies's skill as an architect and designer.
                                                                                                                                                                                     


-Set Design-

More


                                                                                                                                                                                     
The overall set colors and arrangment were inspired by the four types of marble used thoughout the pavilion's construction: Roman Travertine, Green Alpine, Ancient Green Greek and Golden Onyx. To simplify the number of colors (and cost), I opted to reduce the total number down to 3, with the green representing both the Green Alpine and Ancient Green Greek Marble. The novelties were based on significant features and moments in the current pavilion in addition to archived documents and drawings. As such, the novelties feature drawing sections and details, Georg Kolbe's sculpture Dawn, marble patterns and the like. In keeping with the Modernist basis, the choice was made to use icon modifiers, but I wanted to put a bit more fun in the set with some more elaborate novelty art.

November Update:

So, caps were sent to Nebulant for testing, with the following data:

Alphas:



Accents:



When looking at the data, the number that is most relevant is the DeltaE(2000), as that shows the overall colour match, including lightness. Based on the results, GMK is generally very close. A deviation of <2 DeltaE(2000) is indistinguishable to the human eye, while a deviation of 2-10 is considered perceivable under close observation.
If you're looking for a more detailed explanation, it can be found here: https://zschuessler.github.io/DeltaE/learn/

Based on evaluation and the data I've approved the Alphas, but have asked for an R2 for the accents. However, considering how close GMK is on the Accents, there may be some pushback from them, but I believe that its worth the second round. Looking at them next to a colour swatch though, its slightly darker and less saturated, but still within what I'd consider a pretty good tolerance. When put next to the Alphas, the difference is almost indistinguishable, so differences are minimal at best.

I also confirmed with Vala, and deskmat samples are supposed to be coming to them, so hopefully those can be approved soon.

March Update:

Deskmat samples!


Deskmat samples arrived a while ago, and generally, the printing and overall quality are very good, with the one exception being the marble texture mat, as its somewhat blurry at close range- I'm working with Vala on sorting that out with a higher resolution file. The stitching on the inlay mat will be changed to something a bit more muted, as the sample's gold is quite bright right now, and doesn't look like the artwork brass accents. The line stitching will also be changed to something darker to more closely align with the printing

With GMK, I have received production renders, which have been reviewed and sent back for edits, as GMK had doubled the tilde key on the basekit (2 mod colored ones, missing the alpha color). Production renders for the other kits have been reviewed and approved.

I am still waiting for the R2 accent brown samples to be produced and sent to me. Considering that all the other pieces for GMK are now complete (packaging etc.), I'm hopeful that I'll be able to approve the R2 brown and get the set into the production queue. I'm not going to comment on the fulfillment dates, since as we all know, those are entirely at the mercy of GMK. I'm reviewing submissions pretty much as soon as I get them, so I'm waiting along with you guys for them to move things along.

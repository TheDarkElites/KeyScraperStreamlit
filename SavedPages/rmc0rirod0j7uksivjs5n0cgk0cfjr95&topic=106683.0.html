Atlas 5x12 ortholinear board


Live now on CannonKeys.com  and MyKeyboard.EU

100 FCFS on Cannon Keys and 50 FCFS on MyKeyboard



For those having trouble with firmware, please see THIS post

Plate File: HERE



Specs:
  • 5x12 ortholinear layout
  • hotswap - full grid, MIT (1, 2u spacebar, and 2, 2u spacebar options)
  • top mount with no seams (PCB and plate slide up through the bottom similar to the Neuron)
  • bottom plate also curves up into the back (similar to Keycult No. 2 rev.1 TKL)
  • LED acrylic diffusers on front and sides for LED underglow
  • 2 degree angle - mostly so the pen rail can be more functional (angle helps keep pen on the board
  • QMK compatible and open source PCB

Typing test at:
https://www.twitch.tv/apiarykeyboards/


Price: $350 - which includes:

  • main aluminum body
  • brass* bottom lid
  • brass* plate
  • brass* pen rail
  • 3 acrylic diffusers (1 on each side and 1 on front)
  • hotswap PCB and daughterboard with cable
  • bumpons and all necessary hardware
  • vendor stickers 
*all brass is treated and sandblasted

Add-ons:
  • PC plate: $40
  • Extra PCB and daughterboard: $55


Colors available:
  • e-white (additional $20)*
  • navy*
  • grey - matched to Pantone 432c as closely as possible
*white and navy should closely match the same colors on the Satisfaction75


GB will be open for two weeks or until all units are sold out. Fulfillment is 6 months from the end of the GB (December).

Typing test and build stream on Apiary's Twitch channel: https://www.twitch.tv/videos/633899272

I've been reading into whats going on at the Port of LA since that seems like where most of our boards are right now, and thought I'd write them down here for anyone who was curious as to why our keebs are hanging out off the coast of Cali.

Covid-19 and lockdown order surpluses over the last year have congested an already strained system at the Port of LA. It seems like although it wasn't an exceptional increase in shipping volume - take what was added, plus an even larger increase in orders for the holidays, and something like half of the Ports dockworkers on leave for Covid-19 or just quarantine - its keeping boats waiting at anchor for around 8 days before they can dock. I believe I read that there are some 30 ships currently waiting at sea, with over a dozen more coming in within the next few days.

I'm not entirely sure how long it would take shipments to be sent off once a boat does dock, but I'd assume the backups just continue in processing. That all doesn't sound very cheery for the wait time, but it helps me to know whats going on.

Onyx - FRL TKL with a macro column











Designed by funderburker & quaddepos, PCB design by Upas!

Quaddepos (the co-designer behind GMK Minimal and Perestroika, to name a couple) reached out to me about a layout he had wanted to see so we put our heads together and came up with Onyx! Something new yet classical and designed without any compromises!

If you'd like to discuss Onyx, you're welcome to join our Discord servers:
  • Quaddepo's OC Lab Discord server to follow Onyx updates and discussion.
  • funderburker's house Discord server to discuss Onyx and also follow my upcoming projects!

Features
  • Gasket mounted plate
  • Seamless, internally screwed case design
  • 7 degree typing angle
  • VIA-compatible PCB with USB-C daugtherboard, designed by Upas
  • ANSI & ISO layout support, WK & WKL
  • Internally screwed minimal weight design

GB date and time, Vendors and Quantities

Group buy is already scheduled to happen Feb 20th - 26th!

We are happy to announce that we've partnered with 6 vendors to offer Onyx worldwide!
  • Cannon Keys - US
  • DeskHero - Canada
  • Oblotzky Industries - Europe
  • proto[Typist] - UK
  • iLumkb - Asia
  • Daily Clack - Oceania





Case, Colour and Material options

Onyx will be offered in both WK and WKL top variants, with aluminium top/bottom & brass weight. Designed by Europeans,
Onyx also supports both ANSI and ISO layouts with their own separate plates (ISO plates will only be offered by ISO-centric vendors). No universal plates here!

Layout support
More


Plate options
  • Full plate options - Aluminium, Polycarbonate, Brass
  • Half-plate options - Aluminium

Case colour options (Navy Blue added!)
  • Regular - Silver top/Red bottom case with Red aluminium plate; Black case with Black aluminium plate; Green case with Black aluminium plate; Navy Blue case with Black aluminium plate
  • Premium - E-white with Bandblasted Brass plate

Form about case colour and plate options

Included in base orders, Add-ons and Extras

All base orders will come with:
  • Onyx FRL TKL keyboard with aluminium/brass plate & brass weight
  • PCB, USB-C daughterboard and JST cable
  • Gaskets and mounting hardware
  • Cannon Keys keyboard carrying case & cloth

Besides base orders, you'll have the option to purchase:
  • Extra PCBs
  • Extra plates of your choice
  • Gaskets

Quote from: sacklunch on Tue, 09 February 2021, 20:12:00
This is a beautiful board, but I have to ask: why in god's sweet boobies does it cost so much?? How is it that Drop can sell the same kind of board, using the same kind of materials, for 1/3 of the proposed price? Seriously, I am curious.
Well, I guarantee that anything Drop sells isn't even in the same ballpark with regards to material cost or quality as this.
Saying "it's the same kind of materials" is like comparing a soda can to an engine block because they both contain aluminum.
There's no Drop boards with a brass weight or plate.
There's no Drop boards that are gasket mounted.
There's no Drop boards with a layout like this.
Drop boards are mass manufactured to reduce costs. This board is very limited in quantity and as such costs more to produce per unit solely based on volume not even considering material costs, machining costs, etc.

This is a nice design and pretty on point with regards to pricing vs other GB boards in it's class.

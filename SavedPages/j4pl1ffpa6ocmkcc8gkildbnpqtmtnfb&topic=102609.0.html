« on: Wed, 25 September 2019, 09:15:54 »






Google form for placing orders

Order information

Follow me on Instagram for most prompt updates: https://www.instagram.com/fox_lab_keyboard/

Key 65 is featured with the three-layer case and dual-gasket structure. The plate mounting system is an improved version of the gasket sandwich on Leaf 60. In addition, we add gaskets between the middle case and the bottom case. There's an optional rubber sheet between the PCB and the plate. The numerous options will offer you the most freedom to make the keyboard look and feel as you wish.

This GB is the most sophisticated one we've run so far. It could also be the most sophisticated GB in the keyboard history. For each of the three layers of the case, you can choose from 3 materials, 3 finishes, and 9 colors. There are two versions of weights and 6 versions of the top cases. If we count all the options available to choose, the number of possible combinations is around 400 million. We are crazy enough to run the GB in this way. Now we invite you to be a part of this historical crazy GB.

Price:
  • The price for the regular kit is $295 excluding PayPal fee and shipping
  • Upgrade to brass plate: $27
  • Upgrade to titanium plate: $40
  • Upgrade to brass weight: $35
  • Extra PCB: $36
  • The estimated shipping fee for a regular kit is $40. You'll be inviced again before shipping.

Timeline:
  • GB opens for two weeks.
  • The lead time is estimated to be 3 months after GB closes. There might be delays due to the complexity of this GB.

Q: What does the XXX combination will look like? Can you make a prototype or render with this combination?
A: No. I understand it's not easy to imagine how your combination will look like and it's important to you. I'll update some more photos and renders in one day or two. However, we are not able to do this for everyone. I suggest you choose the same color for the top and middle cases.

A: WKL cases have the left blocker and WK cases don't.
6.25u spacebar layout -> choose WK top case
7u spacebar layout with left blocker -> choose WKL top case

About the PCB reset button:

If the PCB you received has a physical button, it may not work after fully assembled. It happened because the PCB supplier changed the BOM after the prototype was tested. The button will be pushed against. the bottom case so the PCB doesn't work. We have removed the physical reset buttons for most PCBs. Only the first 40 or so PCBs still have the physical reset button. You can simply remove it with a wirecutter or soldering iron. It's not a necessary part for the PCB (Leaf60 PCB didn't even have the button). If it bothers you, I can issue a full refund&return. 


Update on the firmware:

I've tested the VIA firmware. It works great.
  • Download QMK toolbox.
  • Download the Key65 VIA firmware (the hex file) here: https://caniusevia.com/docs/download_firmware/
  • Flash the VIA hex file using the QMK toolbox. You need to press Fn+ESC before the flashing.
  • Download VIA: https://github.com/the-via/releases/releases
  • Open VIA then it should detect your keyboard.

Inspiration & Design

More



The inspiration for this set comes from the Japanese high quality craftsmanship. Japanese craftsmanship is handmade and created by people using skills passed down for generations to ensure only the best quality product - a true masterpiece. With that spirit in mind I designed a keycap set, capturing the unique aesthetics of the Japanese culture. The Katakana legends will give the set a very unique look, paired with a beautiful rich blue.


This will be the very first GMK set with Katakana only alphas and mods. So new molds will be created for this set. Because of GMK's limited offering of stock blue colours, this set will be using a custom blue (RAL). The Katakana legends are as you noticed not narrow as the Hiragana mono/sublegends and Katakana sublegends. Instead, the Katakana monolegends are wider to offer a similar look to the latin/cyrillic counterpart. They look like they came straight from GMK. The Katakana-only mods are completely new, my own creation, and were inspired by various old boards. I wanted a more consistent look for the keyset as a whole as the Kanji were left out completely. The keyset has traditional icon mods and text+icon mods, and I think this solution will satisfy nearly every person. I also included all keys for a true ISO and proper ISO Japanese layout (R4 ロ) unlike past sets with japanese legends. The layout was designed on the basis of today's Mac keyboards in Japan but without the JIS bottom row.

Hello Mizuyoukan,

Excuse my jumping in, and thank you for your input on the modifier legends in this set.  Like you, I am a JIS user and joined this community just a week or so ago in order to try to raise awareness of issues related to proper JIS support, as we are very much a minority!

As I see it, there are two parts to having good JIS support in a layout: the alphas, and the modifiers.  From the point of view of the alphas, there are three extra keys in JIS when compared with ANSI; that is ー, む, and ろ in a kana set like this one.  ISO has a key in the same position as む, and it also has an extra key on the bottom row, although in the case of ISO it is placed to the left rather than the right.  The key to the left of backspace, ー, is lacking in both ISO and ANSI.  I believe Masterpiece provides excellent support for all three of these keys.

The other side of JIS support is the modifiers: these are the 変換/無変換 buttons on either side of the space bar (英数/かな on the Mac), plus the 全角/半角 and ひらがな/かたかな buttons on the PC layout.  In order to accommodate these, the space bar must be made small enough to add two or three more keys on the bottom row.  Exacerbating this problem is the fact that every JIS layout is different! The Filco Minila / GH60 require a 3U space, the HHKB is I think 3.25U, the Archiss Maestro 2S is something like 4 or 4.5... the list goes on.  It is thus very difficult for keyset designers to support a full JIS bottom row.

Because of this ambiguity, I am still working on recommendations to give to designers with regards JIS modifier support.  I want to find a set of suggestions which will be useful for a wide range of JIS layouts but not require designers to add too many keys.  Until I can do that, I am focusing my attention on JIS alpha key support, which Masterpiece already covers very well.

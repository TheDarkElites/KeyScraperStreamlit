[GB] bully - a base-kit compatible 40% keyboard | some units shipping now!
« on: Wed, 11 January 2023, 02:00:07 »

"where i'm from, bullies get bullied"

bully is a 40% keyboard designed by phoenix and I of mkh studio. Its 13.5u WK layout is designed to be physically and row-compatible with base keycap sets that are missing minimal 40% keyboard support ("3-key"). Inspired by many great and odd boards I have loved or longed for, it has a comfortably low typing angle of 4.0 degrees, modest design sensibilities, and the pleasant vibes of a firm plateless mount. It is also inspired by me buying a bunch of keysets that don't have 40's support built into them. We hope you like it.

vendors
coffee break keyboards
price: $199.99 USD

Preorders are live from January 13th 9:00 AM PST on cbkbd, with unlimited preorder slots until January 30th, at which point the manufacturing orders will be placed, and a few limited pre-order slots will remain. You can expect this to fulfill by Late Q1/Early Q2 2023. Updates will be provided here, but if you want to see less serious updates, you can get the bully role and join #bully over in the 40% Keyboards discord.

spec

pcb tadpole mount
More

4 degree typing angle
22.4mm ekh (explanation)
8mm/3mm bezels
aluminium case featuring alignment keys and a recessed USB port
1.6mm FR4 PCB, with APM32F072 MCU, compatible with QMK/VIAL (QMK PR)
C3 Unified Daughterboard
KLE


kit contents
1x PCB
1x JST-SH cable, 50mm
1x daughterboard
10x 50A durometer Geonworks Tadpoles
10x 70A durometer Geonworks Tadpoles
A top and bottom case
some SKUF feet
0x plate

PCB edge cuts and plate files will be provided publicly (linked in this thread, my Github, this site, and the #bully channel in the 40% discord) later.

The top and bottom case colors are available in:
Silver/clear

Lilac (ano sample)

good news and bad news
1. Cases are at the local mail center for cbkbd to pick up, pcb's aren't far behind.
2. We didn't do due diligence on C3 daughterboard manufacturing, and I'll take responsibility on this one, I should have brought this up with alas before orders got put in. Long and short of it is that daughterboards were manufactured without the ferrite bead. It's a major pain in the ass for Alas to solder on all of these individually, so here's what we're going to do.
2a. Alas is going to bridge the connection where the ferrite bead was, so the daughterboard has a path to ground, just without the EMI mitigation that the ferrite bead provides. Daughterboard should be functional at that point.
2b. bully units will ship with a ferrite bead (they're  already with Alas at this point), for if you would like the protection it provides and can solder it on yourself. I'll add this step to the informal build guide.
If these measures aren't satisfying enough, well, yell at me here and I'll see if I can arrange for something else.

* mfchill has offered to do the ferrite bead soldering on all our daughterboards. I'm not sure how you do this digitally, but if the opportunity comes,  give him a pat on the back or something, we are extremely grateful for this.
* Cases have arrived, and anodization quality is below what we are comfortable with shipping out. While we ran QC on units from factory photos, we remarked that some of the cases looked like they had been affected by noticeable streaking, and our manufacturer representative assured us that they would be re-finished to meet our quality expectations. On unboxing the crate, it looks like they had re-finished the specific units we had pointed out, and not the rest of the batch. We've weighed our options, and have elected to pay to have Jake of P3D Store kote them in the colors they were manufactured in (he will be handling the matching). If you ordered black, you get black, if you ordered blue you get blue, and so on, no swaps, no custom colors per-customer. This means there's going to be another inevitable delay involved as units get sent over to Jake to be re-finished and back, as were not comfortable with shipping out something that looks like brushed aluminum. This is frustrating for us as it is for you, but we're trying our best to get something on your desk that isn't disappointing.
I would ask that you not send Jake or Alas a deluge of DMs asking about if you can get your case upgraded to a different color, as I don't want to volunteer Jake and Alas for any extra accounting+tracking, and I don't want to make any promises that other people have to work toward keeping. By my understanding, this is a business-to-business transaction, no room for customization of any kind.

Thank you all for your patience and bearing with us.
